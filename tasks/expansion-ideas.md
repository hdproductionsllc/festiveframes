# Festive Frames — Post-July-4 Expansion Brainstorm

Captured 2026-06-11. David's seed ideas plus Claude's additions. Nothing here is
committed roadmap — it's the idea bank to prioritize from after launch.

**Head start:** `src/data/sets/` already contains 17 designed tile sets
(christmas, halloween, thanksgiving, valentines, st-patricks, easter, hanukkah,
diwali, dia-de-muertos, lunar-new-year, kwanzaa, new-years, military,
military-realistic, sakura, essentials, fourth-of-july). Much of the seasonal
expansion below is activation of existing design work, not new design work.

## A. Theme kits (B2C SKUs — same $39 model, new art)

### David's seeds
- **Ethnic heritage / ancestral origin kits** — Irish, Italian, Mexican, Polish,
  German, Greek, Filipino, Indian, Vietnamese... Pattern-driven design (tartan,
  talavera tile, folk embroidery motifs, flag colorways). National flags and
  traditional patterns are generally public domain → NO licensing risk, huge
  identity-purchase energy, parade/festival calendar built in (St. Pat's, Cinco
  de Mayo, Oktoberfest, Columbus/Italian fests, Polish Fest, Juneteenth...).
  STRONG candidate: replicable formula, each kit markets itself inside its
  community. Diwali/Lunar NY/Día de Muertos sets already exist in designer data.
- **Racing / NASCAR-adjacent** — ⚠️ NASCAR, team, and driver marks are heavily
  licensed; do NOT print their marks. The unlicensed version is strong though:
  checkered flags, "RACE DAY", number plates (pick your number), pit-crew
  stripes, dirt-track culture. Sells to the same heartland buyer as July 4 kit.
- **Crossword wings (David's seed — the "NYT" idea)** — wings styled as a
  crossword grid: white tiles, black serif letters, small corner clue-numbers,
  black-square spacer tiles. KEY GEOMETRY: each wing is 5 slots tall = a
  five-letter word reads DOWN each side (Wordle-length — puzzle people will
  feel it). Crossword-as-format is public domain; avoid NYT/Wordle trade dress
  (their logos, the exact green/yellow Wordle colorway). Pairs naturally with
  the letter set below into a "Word Nerd" family. Audience: NYT Games-scale
  puzzle demographic; strong gift positioning ("for the puzzle person who has
  everything"). Could include curated 5-letter word tiles as ready-made strips
  (DRIVE, HONKS, ZOOMS) plus loose letters for spell-your-own.
- **Scrabble / letter set return** — already feature-flagged
  (`ALPHABET_ADDON.enabled`). Relaunch as its own "Say Anything Kit", as the
  add-on again, or bundled with crossword wings as the "Word Nerd Kit".
- **School spirit (generic)** — school-colors kits (maroon/gold, navy/white...)
  with generic mascot icons (eagles, tigers, bulldogs cover most US schools).
  Sold direct = no trademark issue if no school name/logo. Sold THROUGH schools
  = the fundraiser channel below, where the school brings its own identity.

  Halloween kit sells through the same door in October. Start Christian

- **Pet parents** — paw prints, "DOG MOM"/"CAT DAD" slogans. Massive, evergreen.

- **New driver / Sweet 16** — gift moment; parents buying for teens.

## B. Channel plays (B2B — same product, somebody else sells it)

- **Middle/high school fundraisers (David's seed)** — the wrapping-paper /
  discount-card model: school sells school-colors kits at $39+, keeps $10–15/unit.
  Boosters do the selling; school supplies its own mascot identity (kills the
  trademark problem); BUY-ONCE product beats consumables. Pilot with 1–2 local
  STL schools in Aug–Sep. Group-order infrastructure (bulk discount) needed.
- **Car dealerships (David's seed — already in marketing plan)** — dealers
  ALREADY put a branded frame on every car they sell; this upgrades that ritual.
  Co-branded rail + starter tiles, $10–14/frame at volume, or $199–499/mo
  programs. The existing plan estimated $15K/mo at 50 dealers. Pilot: 5 STL
  dealerships.
- **Curated gift catalogs (Claude addition)** — placement play: Uncommon Goods,
  The Grommet, MoMA Design Store, airline catalogs. Premium packaging + story
  ("made in St. Louis") required. Long lead times — apply early.
- **College bookstores** — ⚠️ collegiate marks are licensed (CLC); the
  school-colors-generic version sidesteps it, or pursue CLC licensing later.
- **Youth sports leagues & clubs** — team-colors kits as the new trophy/spirit
  item; same fundraiser mechanics as schools.
- **Realtors & small-business fleets** — logo tiles for fleet vehicles; realtors
  personal-brand their cars already.
- **Corporate gifts / swag** — company-colors kits for sales teams, dealership
  service departments, trade-show giveaways.
- **Festival/event vendors** — wholesale to fair/market booth sellers.

## C. Licensing plays (later — real money, real lawyers)
- NASCAR teams, NFL/MLB/NHL/NBA, colleges (CLC), Disney/anime properties.
  Rule: NEVER print a licensed mark without a deal. Generic adjacents first;
  pursue licenses only once volume proves the category.

## D. Platform plays (the moat)
- **/build designer goes public** — "Design your own kit" premium SKU ($49–59?).
  The tool already exists and is parked. This is the expansion engine: every
  niche above becomes self-serve.
- **Seasonal subscription** — tile-drop club ($9.99/mo or $39.99/yr) — already
  sketched in the marketing plan. The 17 existing sets are a year of drops.
- **Dated limited editions as an annual ritual** — July 4 2026 → Halloween 2026
  → Christmas 2026 → Class of 2027. Scarcity flywheel, repeat buyers.

## PREDICTED BEST-SELLERS (merchandiser ranking, 2026-06-11)

Best sellers need a DEADLINE (holiday urgency), an IDENTITY (buy who you are),
or a GIFTABLE recipient. Ranked:

1. **Christmas Kit** — car-decorating at Christmas is proven mass behavior
   (antlers/wreaths sell millions); this is the premium reusable version.
   Gift season AOV. Set already designed. Deadline+gift+proven behavior.
2. **Halloween Kit** — fastest-growing decor holiday; TRUNK-OR-TREAT is the
   killer use case (parents decorate cars en masse with nothing purpose-built).
   Set already designed. First re-sell to the July 4 list.
3. **Dog Mom / Cat Dad Kit** — strongest identity purchase in merch (~$150B pet
   market); best gift-to-recipient mapping; evergreen, fills seasonal valleys.
   Needs new design.
4. **Game Day Kit (generic city colors)** — already in catalog awaiting
   active:true. WEEKLY seasonality (Aug–Feb) drives the swap habit hardest.
   License-free via colorways.
5. **Veteran / Military Kit** — highest purchase-intent heartland segment,
   gift-driven, near-total overlap with July 4 buyer list. Two sets designed.

Heritage/crossword/school/racing = audience-builders, not chart-toppers.
Fundraisers/dealerships = channel MULTIPLIERS for the five above, not SKUs.

Release calendar: July 4 launch → Game Day (Aug) → Halloween (Oct) →
Christmas (Nov–Dec) → Pet (Jan) → Veteran (May–Jul). Three of six need zero
new design work.

## ALL-CHIPS PICK (decided after platform-level review, 2026-06-11)

**THE RECOGNITION WALL** — donor / memorial / honor walls for churches,
schools, nonprofits, funeral homes. Modular snap-rail wall; tiles = UV-printed
premium blanks (walnut, frosted acrylic, brushed aluminum — plastic is only
the hidden clip/rail skeleton, never the visible face). 13×19 UV bed batches
6–12 tiles per pass; walls assemble to any size.

Why all-chips:
1. **Every sale is an annuity** — institutions add names forever; incumbent
   engraving = weeks + $100s per name; ours = 3 days + ~$30. 10× on speed AND
   price on an already-committed annual purchase.
2. **Ticket fits a solo founder** — $3–10K/install; 5 deals = a real year.
   Tile COGS ~$2 + print + clip.
3. **Triple-leverage on existing bets** — same church/school meetings as the
   Festive Frames fundraiser channel; StillBesideMe is already the memorial
   brand with live fulfillment; David builds software → a wall-management
   portal (admin types name → tile ships) = moat + recurring SaaS-ish layer.
4. **QR becomes profound** — scan a memorial tile → tribute page (SBM
   treatment); donor tile → giving story. No engraver can match it.
5. Validation: one 24×36 pilot section (<$100, a weekend), gifted install in a
   friendly church/school, pro photos, pitch 10 institutions from the photo at
   $3–8K. Close 1 = validated. July 4 launch untouched.

Rejected on the way here: front-door seasonal sign (no universal mount story
David believed; swap habit weaker than assumed), Airbnb welcome boards (good
— mandatory swap per guest turnover, letter set's true market — but no channel
leverage and lower ticket; PARKED not dead), perpetual trophies (folds into
recognition walls as a category), changeable signage (chalkboards good enough).
Key reframes that led here: (a) sell modularity where change is MANDATORY,
not optional; (b) UV printer prints on wood/acrylic/metal — plastic is the
skeleton, never the skin.

## THE BIG FISH (strategic review, 2026-06-11)

**Reframe:** the asset isn't a license plate frame — it's a MODULAR SNAP-TILE
DISPLAY SYSTEM (rail + UV-printed interchangeable tiles + designer + 17 art
sets). The plate frame is canvas #1, not the company.

1. **Custom name tiles — immediate money.** Etsy proves 2× pricing for
   personalization; a custom name strip is the same UV-print workflow as a
   stock slogan. $15–20 checkout add-on = +40% AOV with owned equipment.
   Should ship ASAP, not Q4.
2. **The FRONT DOOR is the bigger canvas.** Seasonal door decor is a massive
   market where the swap habit ALREADY EXISTS (people already rotate wreaths).
   Same buyer, same tiles, same designer, same drops — every kit sells on two
   canvases. No plate laws, no highway engineering, higher price tolerance
   ($59–89), better gift. "Festive Frames" was never a car-specific name.
   ACTION: 1-week door-rail prototype in August + single Etsy listing as a
   demand test before any inventory.
3. **Magnetic mini version** (fridge/locker, ~$19) — gateway/stocking-stuffer;
   kids are natural tile-swappers and become the household swap engine.
4. **Core risk named:** "swap forever" is an UNPROVEN behavior on cars (real
   review: "settling on one" layout). Track QR scans + designer sessions from
   existing customers (weeks 2–6) as the leading indicator ahead of
   repeat-purchase data.
5. **America's 250th is a YEAR-LONG wave** (through 2026 into 2027), not a
   July 4 moment — keep the patriotic line selling at parades/fairs/veteran
   events all year.
6. **Unfair advantage under-used:** David is a pro photographer — content
   production cost ≈ $0 at higher quality than competitors; shift content
   budget toward seeding/capacity.
