# Direct-Print Frame — Ideogram Prompt Pack (Top 6 predicted sellers)

Internal V2 concept art. Generate, drop into `/public/lab/direct-print/<id>.png`,
review in `/lab/direct-print`.

## Ideogram settings (use for every prompt)
- **Style type:** Design (flat graphic). **Magic Prompt:** OFF (keep control).
- **Aspect ratio — BASE:** 16:9 (or custom **1856×1000** ≈ 1.85:1 frame).
- **Aspect ratio — WINGED:** custom **~2064×800** (≈ 2.58:1) or 21:9. Wider = the wings.
- **Center rule (critical):** leave a clean, empty, centered ~2:1 rectangle for the
  license plate. Art lives in the BORDER RING + the wider BOTTOM. Side panels = wings.

## STYLE suffix — append to EVERY prompt
> `Bold flat-vector cartoon sticker art, thick uniform black outlines, vibrant saturated colors, simple cel shading, high contrast, die-cut sticker look, plain solid white background, symmetrical, reads clearly from 15 feet away.`

## NEGATIVE / Exclude field (every prompt)
> `text, letters, numbers, words, watermark, signature, realistic photo, 3d render, cluttered or filled center, anything covering the center rectangle, busy background`

## BONUS — "THE KEEPER" (Dealer Edition) — B2B

**Insight:** dealer ad frames get ripped off because they're ugly free advertising.
The IDEAL dealer frame is one the customer is *proud to keep* → the dealership
gets lasting rolling ads **and** a service/review funnel. Sell co-branded in bulk;
molded cheap at scale; per-dealer logo via UV print.

**What makes it ideal:**
- **Premium look, not plasticky** — matte black + a brushed-metal accent + a thin
  stripe in the dealer's brand color. Feels like an OEM accessory, not a giveaway.
- **Understated branding** in the wide bottom nameplate: small crest + dealer name +
  **city** (local pride = the customer keeps it). Top/side rails stay clean.
- **The ROI killer — a QR** in the bottom corner → **book service / leave a Google
  review / referral / "free first oil change."** Turns the bumper into a measurable
  funnel (service revenue + reviews + referrals), not just brand impressions.
- Tiny "Festive Frames" co-mark → channel growth.
- **Local-pride variant:** big CITY name + skyline silhouette, dealer logo smaller —
  customer reps their town, dealer rides along.

**Ideogram prompt (premium style — NOT the cartoon sticker style):**
> A premium, sophisticated automotive license-plate frame: a sleek matte-black
> border around a large empty centered ~2:1 rectangle (kept completely plain — for a
> license plate). A thin brushed-chrome accent stripe runs along the top and side
> borders. The wider bottom border is a clean nameplate panel with an elegant blank
> emblem/crest centered (placeholder for a dealership logo) and tidy empty space
> beside it, plus a small square reserved in the bottom-right corner for a QR code.
> Modern, high-end, understated, minimalist — the look of a luxury car accessory.
> Plain white background, no text, no lettering. Aspect ratio 16:9.
> _Exclude:_ cartoon, comic, cluttered, neon, garish, text, realistic photo.

**B2B targets first:** performance / EV / off-road / luxury dealers (brand-conscious),
then mainstream. Pitch = "advertising your customers won't throw away + a service
funnel you can measure."

## BONUS — "GAME DAY" (School Spirit Edition) — B2B + identity

**Why it's huge:** people *love* repping their school (HS + college), AND the school
itself becomes your sales channel — sell as a **fundraiser** (a cut to the booster
club / athletic dept) and they market it to every student, parent, and alum for you.
Recurring forever: new students, graduation gifts, homecoming, alumni.

**Two variants:**
- **Mascot Roar (viral):** the school mascot's face wraps the plate (plate = the
  roaring mouth) — most schools have animal mascots (tiger, eagle, bulldog, wildcat,
  ram, panther…), so it plugs into the face/CHOMP mechanic. Wings = mascot ears/horns.
- **Varsity Pride (clean):** collegiate varsity look in two school colors — chevron
  stripe, a blank crest for the logo, a banner for the school name + "EST." year.

**Customization → SKU engine:** school name · mascot type · 2 school colors · line
text ("Proud [Mascot] Mom/Dad", "Class of 20XX", "Alumni", "Student-Athlete") · city.
Greek-life variant = letters. "Proud parent" is a massive buyer segment.

**⚠️ Licensing:** official school/college names, logos, and mascots are usually
TRADEMARKED (collegiate licensing). Two clean lanes: (1) **partner with the school**
(the fundraiser model authorizes their marks), or (2) **generic templates** — team
colors + a generic mascot type + custom text — for DIY without infringing. Don't sell
official marks unlicensed.

**Ideogram prompt — Mascot Roar (generic tiger; swap mascot/colors per school):**

> corners, white fangs lining the inside top and bottom edges of the hole like a roar,
> a blank varsity-style banner ribbon across the wider bottom border (empty, for a
> school name). Athletic game-day energy. (+ append STYLE / EXCLUDE)
> **Winged:** same tiger frame, EXTRA WIDE, with two tiger EARS on the far-left/right
> side panels.

**Ideogram prompt — Varsity Pride (clean):**
> A clean collegiate VARSITY license-plate frame in two bold school colors (navy and
> gold): thick border around a large empty centered ~2:1 rectangle (kept plain — for a
> license plate). A sporty chevron stripe pattern on the border, a blank circular
> emblem at top center (placeholder for a school logo), and a clean blank banner on the
> wider bottom border for a school name. Premium, spirited, athletic. Plain white
> background, no text. 16:9.

**Sell to:** booster clubs, athletic departments, alumni associations, school
bookstores, team parents, Greek life. Pitch = "a fundraiser your whole community
*wants* to buy, and free rolling advertising for the school."
