# Custom frames: how we should use UV printing

_A short memo for the Henry + Bill conversation. Bottom line: we agree on UV
printing — the question is **what we print**, not **how**._

## TL;DR
- ✅ **UV printing is the right method.** Full color, durable, cheap, fast, no
  hand-assembly. Bill's right — this is how we should make custom frames.
- ⚠️ **The risk is printing RAW PHOTOS, not UV printing itself.** At this size and
  viewing distance, photos fail. The fix isn't to drop the photo upload — it's to
  **stylize the uploaded photo, then UV-print the art.** Photo *in*, art *out*.

## Why raw photos fail on a plate frame
1. **Readability at distance (the big one).** A frame is seen from a car-length+
   away, often moving. Photos are detail-dense and low-contrast — shrink one to ~3″,
   view from 20 ft, and it turns to mud. Road signs, logos, and mascots are simplified
   *on purpose* so the eye reads them at a glance. A photo fights that.
2. **The canvas is tiny.** Thin border + ~3″ wings. Photos need size *and* resolution
   to show detail; a face at 3″ is a blob.
3. **Garbage in, garbage out.** Customer uploads are inconsistent — bad light, blur,
   busy backgrounds. A photo pipeline prints all of it → complaints + refunds.
   **Stylization normalizes every upload** into a consistent good-looking result.
4. **Photos read cheap; illustration reads premium** — and a signature illustrated
   style is our brand moat. Photos have no consistent look → they commoditize us.
5. **Outdoor reality.** UV print struggles most with the gradients/skin-tones photos
   are made of; flat bold art prints crisp. Sun-fade + glare ruin a photo subtly but
   a high-contrast cartoon shrugs it off.
6. **Emotional stakes.** A slightly-off photo of someone's dog = anger. A charming
   stylized version is forgiving and often *more* loved — which is why the pet-portrait
   market pays MORE for illustrated portraits than photo prints.
7. **Clean die-cut needs clean edges.** Fur + background photos cut messy; illustration
   gives intentional, cuttable contours.

## The recommendation (gives everyone the win)
**Upload your pet → we stylize it to our house look → UV-print that on the wing.**
Bill keeps the upload magic + the UV process; we get something that reads from a car,
looks premium, prints clean, and stays on-brand.

## How we settle it — with evidence, not opinions
Make **two real samples**: one raw-photo frame, one stylized frame. Mount both on a
car and **photograph from 15–20 ft, in sunlight.** Whichever reads better wins. Reality
ends the debate faster than either of us arguing it.
